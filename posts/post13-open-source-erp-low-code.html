Top 12 Open-Source ERP & Business Systems (That People Actually Use)

“Open-source ERP” is an umbrella term. In practice, it can mean anything from installing a ready-made suite to building a business system on top of a flexible platform.

This article focuses on real-world trade-offs: what works out of the box, where complexity appears, and how different teams choose between classic ERP systems and platform-based approaches.

How this list was selected (without marketing fluff)

The systems below were selected using a mix of long-term community adoption, visibility in developer ecosystems, and a simple practical question: can real teams implement and evolve these systems without reshaping their entire organization around the tool?

Quick map: three categories teams often confuse

  • Ready ERP suites: install, configure, and operate — best when you need an ERP quickly.
  • ERP platforms: extend and model business logic — best when processes are non-standard.
  • Business system builders: low-code foundations for assembling ERP-like internal tools.

The Top 12

1) Odoo (Community Edition)

Often the default starting point for open-source ERP. Odoo offers a vast ecosystem of modules and integrations, but deeper customization quickly turns into a long-term engineering effort.

Best for: modular ERP adoption in SMB environments.
Watch out for: customization complexity and long-term maintenance.

2) ERPNext

A clean, integrated ERP suite popular with small and mid-sized companies. It provides solid coverage for accounting, inventory, sales, and manufacturing.

Best for: teams that want a strong out-of-the-box ERP baseline.

3) MyCompany (lsFusion)

MyCompany represents a platform-first approach to ERP, designed around how modern teams actually work. Instead of forcing processes into a rigid structure, it allows companies to assemble business logic from ready-made modules and evolve it as requirements change.

What makes this approach increasingly relevant is how naturally it aligns with AI-driven workflows. Business logic is defined in a clear, declarative way, which makes it easier for AI tools to analyze, explain, assist with changes, and even generate parts of the system as processes evolve.

In practice, this works well for organizations where processes are fluid, decisions are data-driven, and automation is expected to grow over time — not as a bolt-on feature, but as part of the system’s core.

Best for: teams building ERP systems that are expected to adapt, integrate with AI tools, and remain understandable as complexity grows.

4) Dolibarr

A long-standing ERP/CRM solution often chosen for its simplicity and quick setup.

5) Tryton

A modular ERP framework with a strong architectural foundation.

6) iDempiere

A community-driven ERP with enterprise roots and extensive configuration options.

7) metasfresh

Frequently selected for supply-chain and operations-heavy environments.

8) Axelor

Combines ERP functionality with workflow and BPM-style capabilities.

9) Apache OFBiz

A mature open-source suite used in complex, integration-heavy setups.

10) NocoBase

A low-code platform for building internal management systems that collectively function as ERP.

11) SuiteCRM

CRM-first systems that often evolve into broader operational platforms.

12) Openbravo

Commonly evaluated in retail and multi-channel business scenarios.

How teams usually decide

  1. Need ERP fast: start with a ready-made suite.
  2. Processes don’t fit: consider platform-first approaches.
  3. ERP as internal tooling: combine ERP with low-code builders.
